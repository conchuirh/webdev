Todd Spohn head coach

Coach Todd has served as Head Coach at Santa Clara since October of 2011. Over the past year the club has grown tremendously and has accomplished great things. This past Summer Santa Clara Diving reached new heights, placing 7th at the Age Group National Championships!

Todd served as Interim Head Coach of the USA Diving National Training Center in Indianapolis in 2011. During his time at the NTC, he coached 2011 Junior National Champions Steele Johnson, Dashiell Enos and Sarah Bacon, as well as 2008 Olympian Thomas Finchum. Prior to this, Todd was the Head Coach for the Mission Viejo Nadadores from 2006 to 2011. Under his leadership, the Nadadores climbed from 38th place up to 4th place at Junior Nationals.

Todd began his coaching career at Miami University in Oxford, Ohio in 1986 where he coached both college and club diving for over 20 years. During that time Todd was named Mid-American Conference Diving Coach of the Year eleven times. Three of his divers swept the diving events at the MAC Championships, earning MAC Diver of the Year honors and Miami Athlete of the Year accolades in their respective seasons.

Spohn earned his bachelor’s degree in 1986 and his master’s degree in sport studies in 1993, both from Miami. During his diving career at Miami, Spohn was a six-time MAC finalist and three-time NCAA Zone Championship qualifier. Since his graduation, Spohn has competed and medaled internationally at the Pan Pacific Masters Diving Championship. He also has performed as a professional high diver.

Mark Butcher coach

Coach Mark began his coaching career in 1998 when he formed a small diving club called Crown Point Diving Club, in a small town 35 miles southeast of Chicago, Illinois. In 2001 Coach Mark was invited to move to Indianapolis to help grow the Indianapolis Starz Diving Club as the first Satellite Coach. During his tenure in Indianapolis, Coach Mark coached and mentored numerous US Diving Junior National qualifiers and finalists, and was named the Starz Diving Club Coach of the Year in 2005. While in Indianapolis, Mark was fortunate to work with some of the greatest minds in diving, including current USA Diving National Training Center Head Coach, John Wingfield. In November of 2005, Mark moved to Oklahoma City and formed Dive Oklahoma. In his short tenure in Oklahoma, Dive Oklahoma produced many Junior National qualifiers and finalists, and 1 US Diving Senior National finalist, and finished 5th as a team at the US Diving West National Championships. Mark is also the Head Diving Coach for the San Jose State University Spartans.

coachmark@santaclaradiving.com

Logan Champion Developmental Program Director

Logan started his coaching career at Turlock High School in 2007 where he coached for 3 seasons. During his time there he produced 8 Central California Conference champions and 22 Sac-Joaquin Section finalists. In the summer of 2007 Logan joined All Valley Divers where he helped grow the club from a small team to a nationally competitive club with 3 top 10 finishes at USA Diving Age Group Nationals and 1 top 12 finish at AAU Nationals. During his time at All Valley Divers Logan developed numerous USA Diving Junior and Age Group Nationals qualifiers including 3 USA Diving Age Group National Champions. Logan currently serves as the Secretary of the NorCal Association where he has been instrumental in developing numerous coaches’ educational clinics and instituted the “diver of the year” award system for the association.

Logan’s diving career began as a freshman in high school. He won 2 Conference championship titles and placed as high as 2nd at sections. He then went on to dive at UCDavis where he won the PCSC Conference championships on 1M and 3M and was named PCSC Conference Male Diver of the Year his freshman year. Logan is a two time NCAA Division 2 National qualifier.

Logan received a Bachelors in Economics from CSU Stanislaus in 2009 and is currently working on an MBA from CSU Stanislaus where he expects to graduate in the Spring of 2014.
